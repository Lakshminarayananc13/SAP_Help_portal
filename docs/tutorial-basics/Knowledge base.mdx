# Knowledge Base Overview

A **Knowledge Base** is a collection of documents that captures and organizes helpful information. Knowledge bases are valuable because they make information easy to find, share, and improve over time.

## Benefits of a Knowledge Base

###  Searchable Content

Users can quickly search for knowledge base documents and find the information they need.

###  Collaboration and Contributions

After finding a document, users can:

- Add comments and feedback.
- Edit the document (if they have the required permissions).
- Contribute improvements to future versions.

If a document publisher incorporates a user's comment into a later version, the user may receive credit as a contributor.

###  Rich Media Support

Knowledge base documents can include:

- Images
- Screenshots
- Diagrams
- Videos (if supported)

###  Related Resources

Each knowledge base document can contain:

- Related links
- Reference materials
- Additional resources for deeper learning

###  Contributor Information

Knowledge base documents often include a list of contributors, such as:

- Community members whose posts or comments were used
- Authors who created the document
- Editors who reviewed and refined the content

## Why Use a Knowledge Base?

A knowledge base helps organizations and communities:

- Centralize information
- Improve collaboration
- Reduce duplicate questions
- Share best practices
- Preserve institutional knowledge

---

> A well-maintained knowledge base enables users to find accurate information quickly and contribute to continuous improvement.
